    > Online ICT learning refers to the process of acquiring knowledge, skills, and competencies related to information and communication technology through the use of online resources and platforms.

    > Online ICT learning typically involves the use of digital tools such as videos, interactive simulations, online courses, and virtual classrooms to deliver content and facilitate learning.

    > Online ICT learning can cover a wide range of topics, including computer programming, web design, cybersecurity, digital marketing, data analysis, and more. It is often self-paced, meaning that learners can complete the course or program at their own speed, and can access the learning materials from anywhere with an internet connection.

    > Online ICT learning is becoming increasingly popular due to its convenience, flexibility, and accessibility. It allows learners to acquire new skills and knowledge without having to attend traditional classroom-based courses, and it can be more affordable than in-person training.

    > Additionally, online ICT learning can be tailored to individual learning needs and preferences, allowing learners to focus on specific areas of interest or skill gaps.
